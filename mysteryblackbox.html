The Mystery Black Box

What Did We Do?

Mr. Sinusas’s Mystery Black Box had an opening to pour water in. When you poured water into it, water would come out, and the output amount varied every day. Sometimes it would be more than the amount poured in, sometimes less, sometimes equal. Each group had to theorize what the mechanism inside was, make a model of it, and then a 3D animation in a software called Autodesk Maya. I first made a sketch of what I thought was inside using Adobe Photoshop and soon made a 2D animation showcasing the movement of water through the box. Then, my group and I discussed our theories and created a cardboard model of what we theorized. We used a tipping bucket connected to a funnel that pours water out of the box. It worked as we hoped, and we started working on our animations in Maya. Part of my animation is below, but the full rendered animation is unavailable as I was not able to finish rendering it in time. During this project, I learned how to analyze data by looking at past inputs and outputs of water into and from the black box, and using that data to collaborate with my group effectively.

What did I learn??

I demonstrated my problem solving and critical thinking skills during this project by analyzing data and thinking about what mechanisms could be inside the box and troubleshooting issues my group and I faced along the way. We faced multiple problems with water leakage, bucket balance, and more, and they lead to many spills. However, my group and I overcame these issues and came up with solutions and fixes. We used critical thinking skills to figure out what was inside, and we used our problem solving skills to fix the road blocks we encountered.
