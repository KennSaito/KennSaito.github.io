Biotech Breakthroughs

What Did We Do?

The Biotech Breakthroughs experiment was done to analyze students' DNA and see which of our peers are closest to us in terms of VNTRs (Variable Number Tandem Repeats). We used a series of processes to extract and analyze our genetics, involving PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction), and Gel electrophoresis. Then, we analyzed the results measuring how far our DNA travelled across a gel matrix and put the results into a spreadsheet to calculate the size of our DNA fragment. The sizes were compared among classmates and ended up showing us that race has no part in how related we are genetically, proving that it is a social construct. We then created a documentary for the project, with each member doing one part of the video. My job was to edit the initial interview where one student would explain the importance of DNA extraction and PCR testing, and I helped with making a stop motion video to explain the structure of DNA.
